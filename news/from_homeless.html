From Homeless to Helping Others Get Off the Streets

Summary

Paul Williams had nowhere to sleep, had been addicted to drugs and had been in and out of jail. Then he found Chrysalis. Chrysalis offers programs and training aimed at helping homeless and low-income people find jobs. It opened in 1984, and since then has served more than 60,000 individuals from centers in Los Angeles’s Skid Row, in Santa Monica and in Pacoima in the San Fernando Valley.
